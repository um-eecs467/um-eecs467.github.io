# EECS467 Design Project 

The goal of this part of the lab will be to work in teams and propose, implement, and demonstrate a self-determined design project. A project that integrates sensing, planning, and acting to make changes in the environment using physical robots (e.g., the MBot, a robot arm, etc.) is desired. 

# Teams

You will form your own teams for the design project (you may stay in the same group as Botlab or form new groups). 3-4 persons per group. Please sign up in the [google sheet](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1uVVT4hiflUZ8R37HQyZyvweCKAxml9SA768MLY6bXeE/edit?usp=drive_link) under **Design\_Lab** tab after your group is formed (Please sign up by Feb.27, 2026; afterwards the instructors will create teams if you have not yet formed groups). 

Each team proposes a project (oral and written), sets milestones to track its own progress, submits a progress report (status update presentation), prepares for and gives a final public demonstration, and submits a written final report.

# Questions to Consider

When forming your groups or discussing ideas with your teammates, consider these questions:  
Project Title \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_

1. Describe the project in one sentence.  
2. What problem does this project address? What are the stakeholders?  
3. What compelling new capability will result from solving this problem?  
4. How would a solution to this problem be verified?  
5. What are some of the existing methods in the literature to solve this problem (if any)? Will your solution be any different/novel?  
6. What are the main foreseeable challenges for this project?  
7. How would you mitigate these challenges?

Additionally, consider the budget/cost/timeline/materials.

# Proposal 

# Each team will present your proposed project.

**Oral presentation requirement (Due March 20, 2026, 3:30PM EST) :** Prepare a one-slide poster (see folder for a basic [template](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1pHw_AqpTMg-ZEoB6OkeoDSu0igqCGu42/view?usp=drive_link)) and give an oral presentation (8-10 min) to the class describing your project. Be sure to include answers to all the questions above. Please submit your poster/presentation files to [Google Drive folder](https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/1gok2X2SQv1SghlqkkFWbf8yQBMK32GYN?usp=drive_link).  
**Written proposal (Due March 22, 2026, 11:59pmEST):** 2-column [IEEE style](https://www.ieee.org/conferences/publishing/templates.html). ([overleaf template](https://www.overleaf.com/project/5c9525ca4f69c335ea1c2dc2)) Include the following components:   
Abstract \- summary of your project’s aims and objectives, proposed solutions, etc.  
Keywords \- keywords  
Introduction \- give an overview of the problem; motivate why it is important/necessary  
Related Work \- describe existing methods that solve this problem  
Proposed Method \- describe your proposed solution/system. What’s the proposed system design? How would you conduct experiments? You may also discuss foreseeable challenges and possible solutions in this section.  
Proposed Evaluation \- describe your proposed evaluation method – how would you determine your project is successful? Any quantitative/qualitative metrics? Expected outcome?  
Timeline \- Plan milestones and timeline.  
Budget \- describe your budget and any materials required.  
References – citations

No strict page limit for the written proposal, but after including all of the above components it’ll probably be \>1 pages at least. Please submit the written proposal to Canvas.
